# Understanding Lenses

Lenses are **7 different ways to look at the same capacity data**. The capacity page fetches all data in a single API call; switching lenses is instant (client-side, no additional loading).

One overlay lens can be active at a time, and the **Planned** base layer is always visible.

## How Lenses Work

Each lens controls what appears in the four capacity page components:

| Component | What Changes Per Lens |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **KPI Strip** | Different summary metrics displayed |
| **Heatmap Tooltip** | Additional overlay values in the tooltip |
| **Chart** | Overlay line/bar added on top of base demand/capacity |
| **Table** | Additional columns appear for the active lens |

A lens is **enabled** (clickable) when its data exists in the current date range. If a lens button appears disabled/grayed out, it means no data of that type has been configured for the visible period.

---

## Planned (Blue) — Default

**Always available.** Shows base work-package demand vs. shift capacity.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Average Utilization, Peak Utilization, Capacity Gap (MH), Total Demand MH, Total Capacity MH
- **Heatmap:** Utilization color per (date, shift) cell
- **Chart:** Demand bars vs. capacity line
- **Table:** Date, Total Demand, Total Capacity, Utilization %, Gap, per-shift breakdown

### Best For

Day-to-day staffing decisions. "Do we have enough people on each shift this week?"

---

## Allocated (Amber)

**Enabled when:** Demand allocations exist in Admin > Capacity > Demand Allocations.

Shows customer contract minimums overlaid on demand. Use this to verify that contracted hours are being met.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Total Allocated Demand MH (in addition to planned KPIs)
- **Chart:** Amber dashed overlay line showing allocated demand level
- **Table:** "Allocated MH" column per date showing the effective demand after allocations

### How Allocations Affect Demand

- **MINIMUM_FLOOR:** If WP demand is below the contracted floor, the system raises effective demand to the floor
- **ADDITIVE:** Extra MH are added on top of WP demand

The "Allocated MH" column shows the effective demand after allocation logic is applied. Compare it to the base "Demand MH" column to see the impact.

### Best For

Contract compliance. "Are we meeting DHL's guaranteed 8 MH/day?"

---

## Events (Sky)

**Enabled when:** Flight events exist in Admin > Capacity > Flight Events.

Shows flight arrivals and departures with coverage window counts.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Event count, Coverage window count for the date range
- **Table:** "Events" column showing the number of flight events per date
- **Chart:** No overlay line (events are point-in-time counts, not a time series of MH)

### Coverage Windows

Each event generates arrival and/or departure coverage windows — time periods when staff must be available. The event count in the table helps identify high-traffic days.

### Best For

Checking staffing around arrivals and departures. "Which days have the most flight activity?"

---

## Forecast (Teal)

**Enabled when:** Forecast rates have been generated from an active model in Admin > Capacity > Rate Forecasts.

Shows projected future demand based on historical patterns.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Total Forecasted Demand MH
- **Chart:** Teal dotted overlay line showing forecasted demand
- **Table:** "Forecast MH" column per date

### Reading the Forecast

The forecast line represents what the algorithm **predicts** demand will be, based on the configured model (moving average, weighted average, or linear trend). Compare it against:

- The blue demand bars (actual WP demand) for dates with data
- The capacity line for future dates to see if projected demand fits within capacity

### Best For

Future planning. "Based on the last 30 days, what should we expect next week?"

---

## Worked (Green)

**Enabled when:** Time bookings exist in Admin > Capacity > Worked Hours.

Shows actual hours worked compared to planned demand.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Total Worked Hours, Plan vs. Actual variance (%)
- **Chart:** Green solid overlay line showing actual worked hours
- **Table:** "Worked MH" column per date

### Variance

- **Positive variance** — planned more than worked (over-estimated)
- **Negative variance** — worked more than planned (under-estimated)

### Best For

Productivity analysis. "Did we actually use the hours we planned? Are our estimates accurate?"

---

## Billed (Indigo)

**Enabled when:** Billing entries exist in Admin > Capacity > Billed Hours.

Shows invoiced hours compared to worked and planned hours.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Total Billed Hours, Worked vs. Billed variance (%)
- **Chart:** Indigo solid overlay line showing billed hours
- **Table:** "Billed MH" column per date

### Variance

- **Positive variance** (worked > billed) — under-billing, leaving revenue on the table
- **Negative variance** (billed > worked) — over-billing, potential audit risk

### Best For

Revenue reconciliation. "Are we invoicing accurately for the work performed?"

---

## Concurrent (Purple)

**Enabled when:** Flight events with arrivals/departures exist (same data as Events lens, but analyzed differently).

Shows how many aircraft overlap on the ground simultaneously.

### What You See

- **KPIs:** Peak concurrent aircraft, Average concurrent aircraft
- **Chart:** No overlay line (aircraft count and MH are different units — they can't share the same axis)
- **Table:** "Peak Concurrent" and "Avg Concurrent" columns per date

### Why It Matters

High concurrency means multiple aircraft need attention at once. Even if total MH fits within capacity, having 5 aircraft arrive within the same hour creates coordination challenges that raw MH numbers don't capture.

### Best For

Understanding workload intensity. "How many aircraft are we juggling at once during peak hours?"

## Lens Comparison Matrix

| Lens | Overlay Color | Chart Overlay | Table Column(s) | Enables When |
|------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Planned | Blue | *(base layer)* | Demand, Capacity, Utilization, Gap | Always |
| Allocated | Amber | Dashed line | Allocated MH | Allocations exist |
| Events | Sky | None | Events count | Flight events exist |
| Forecast | Teal | Dotted line | Forecast MH | Forecast rates generated |
| Worked | Green | Solid line | Worked MH | Time bookings exist |
| Billed | Indigo | Solid line | Billed MH | Billing entries exist |
| Concurrent | Purple | None | Peak/Avg Concurrent | Flight events exist |

## Tips

- **Start with Planned** to understand basic demand vs. capacity
- **Add lenses progressively** as you enter more data types
- **Compare Worked vs. Planned** to validate your estimation accuracy
- **Use Forecast** for dates beyond your current work package data
- **Check Concurrent** during high-traffic periods to understand coordination complexity
